CHAPTER ZERO — THE NIGHT EVERYTHING BURNED
It was the smell that woke you.

Not smoke. Smoke has a kind of warmth to it.
This was burning flesh.

The merchant caravan you had been guarding — twelve wagons, thirty-one people — was GONE.

Not robbed. Not scattered.
Gone.

Ash and charred bone were all that remained. And a single black arrow lodged in the earth, inches from your sleeping head.
⚠ SOMEONE LEFT YOU ALIVE. ON PURPOSE. ⚠
A warning. Or an invitation.

Among the wreckage, something moved — a boy, no older than twelve. His left hand was gone at the wrist. Cauterized. Deliberately.

"They took my sister," he whispered. "The men with the black crests. They laughed while they did it."

He pressed something into your hand. A cloth badge. Black. A serpent eating its own tail.
☠ YOU RECOGNIZE THE SYMBOL — OROCHI CLAN ☠
The most feared criminal syndicate in western Japan. They don't leave witnesses. Except when they want someone to suffer.
THE BOY IS DYING. WHAT DO YOU DO?
SCENE II — KUROKAWA
Kurokawa is a village that has forgotten what safety feels like.

The gates are unguarded. The wells are dry. Children do not play in the streets.

An old woman sits outside a burned shrine, cleaning a wound on her forearm with the calm detachment of someone for whom pain has become routine.

"Another wanderer," she says. "They always come. They always leave."

"The Orochi men came three nights ago. Took the young. Left the rest to remember."

She points a crooked finger at the road north. "No one who goes that way comes back."
WHAT MADE PRADEEP A WARRIOR?
SCENE III — THE FIRST GATE
The northern road is quiet. Too quiet.

The Orochi guard at the checkpoint is picking his teeth with a knife. The knife has dried blood on it. He doesn't bother to stand when he sees you.

"Road's closed, foreigner." He grins. Three teeth missing.
"Turn around and I'll only take one hand."

He has two companions behind him. They are not paying attention.
HOW DO YOU PROCEED?
SCENE IV — THE OATH
You stand over the fallen men. The road north stretches into darkness.

Somewhere at the end of it:
Jinpachi. Raijin. Muramasa.
Katsuro with his silver mask.
And behind all of them —
Akuma-no-Ken. The Devil Swordsman.

You clean your blade on the grass. You do not pray. You do not hesitate.
⚔ "Every man who wore that black crest will answer for what they did. Every. Single. One." ⚔

THE LEGEND OF PRADEEP
Akuma-no-Ken crumbles to the snow-covered ground.
His sword clatters. Silence falls over Mount Fuji.

Pradeep looks out over Japan — a land finally free from the shadow of tyrants.
No master. No chains. Only the wind, the blade, and the open road.

He is not Japanese. He carries no clan mon.
But Japan will remember him as one of its greatest samurai.
